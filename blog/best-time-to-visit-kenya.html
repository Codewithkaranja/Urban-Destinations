Best Time to Visit Kenya (Month-by-Month)

Plan the right season for your style: wildlife, beaches, weather, crowds and value — in one simple guide.

Quick answer: what season is best?

If you want the “classic” safari feel with great weather, aim for the drier months. If you want value and fewer crowds, the shoulder seasons can be amazing with the right planning.

Best for safari + weather
  • Often: Jan–Mar and Jul–Oct
  • Clearer roads, comfortable game drives
  • Higher demand in peak months
Best for value
  • Often: Nov and parts of Apr–May
  • Deals possible if you’re flexible
  • Rain can affect some routes/lodges

Month-by-month planning notes

Jan

Warm, generally clear. Great safari + good value if booked smart.

Feb

Excellent wildlife viewing, hot afternoons. Strong month for photographers.

Mar

Good wildlife; rains may begin late month. Plan with a flexible route.

Apr

Often wetter. Great deals if you accept rain risk and choose reliable stays.

May

Still rainy in many areas. Best for low crowds and discounted packages.

Jun

Transition to drier season. A calm month before peak crowds.

Jul

Peak safari season begins. Book early for Mara and popular lodges.

Aug

Very strong sightings, migration excitement. Higher prices + high demand.

Sep

Still excellent safari weather. A sweet spot for clear skies and game drives.

Oct

Great for safari and fewer crowds than peak. Good month for balanced travel.

Nov

Short rains can appear. Often good deals + green scenery.

Dec

Holiday demand rises. Book early and expect busier routes/attractions.

Premium tip: If you want a “luxury feel” without peak-season pressure, consider late Jan–early Mar or Oct.

Best time for beaches (Diani / Coast)

Beach travel is great most of the year. Your main variables are rain and wind. If you want calm beach days, aim for drier stretches and plan activities in the morning.
