Family Safari in Kenya: Kid-Friendly Planning

A calm, safe, and fun safari comes down to pace, the right lodge, and a schedule your kids can actually enjoy.

Start with the right safari pace

The biggest family safari mistake is planning like adults have unlimited energy. Kids do best with shorter game drives, rest time, and fewer transfers.

Premium rule for families: Choose one main park, stay 2 nights minimum, and keep each game drive to a kid-friendly block.

How to choose a kid-friendly lodge/camp

  • Comfortable rooms, clean bathrooms, reliable meals
  • Safe environment for walking around the property
  • Pool or relaxed space for midday breaks
  • Flexible meal timing (helps a lot)

What to pack for kids

  • Light jacket (early drives), hat, sunscreen
  • Snacks and water (keeps moods stable)
  • Small binoculars / a simple “animal checklist” game
  • Motion sickness prep if needed

Safety basics (simple, practical)

  • Stay inside vehicle during sightings unless guide says otherwise.
  • Follow lodge rules, especially at night.
  • Keep valuables minimal on game drives.

Sample 3-day family safari outline

  • Day 1: Travel + settle + short sunset drive (optional)
  • Day 2: Morning drive + rest + evening drive
  • Day 3: Short morning drive + return
